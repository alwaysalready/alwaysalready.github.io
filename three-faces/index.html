The Three Faces of
FinTech

A CHI 2019 Workshop

Financial technology, or fintech, is affording much more than new forms of transaction or new mechanisms for controlling the flow and investment of money. The increasingly connected, infrastructural and data-driven nature of these technologies suggests fundamental transformations in how we make sense of ideas such as value, trust and fairness, and how we put them into practice. This workshop invites participants to put their thoughts and/or work into conversation with one of three ‘faces of fintech’: the technological, the human, or the infrastructural. The aim will be to draw together a deep and cross-cutting discussion about fintech, considering how it is interwoven into wider sociotechnical practices, and the implications for designing responsible human-computer interactions.
